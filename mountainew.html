About the Himalayan Mountains

The Himalayas, often referred to as the "abode of snow," is a vast mountain range in Asia, spanning across several countries including India, Nepal, Bhutan, and China. It is home to some of the world's highest peaks, including Mount Everest.

Impact of Climate on the Himalayas

The Himalayas are highly sensitive to climate change. Rising global temperatures have led to the melting of glaciers, resulting in glacial retreat and the formation of glacial lakes. These changes pose significant challenges to local ecosystems, water resources, and communities that depend on glacial meltwater.
